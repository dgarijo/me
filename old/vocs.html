Vocabularies and ontologies

These are the vocabularies that I have created or contributed to during my research. All of them have been published following the Linked Data principles: they are derreferenceable with content negotiation enabled (RDF/XML). If any of the vocabularies does not work please send me an e-mail.

The Workflow Fragment Description Ontology (2013)

Ontology designed as an extension of P-Plan in order to represent workflow fragments and their relationships to workflow templates

Check it out here!

The Research Object Optimization Ontology (2013)

The Research Object Optimization Ontology (RO-Optimization) is an extension of the Research Object model in order to be able to describe optimizations of workflows and their results.

Check it out here!

The Research Object Models (2012)

Family of vocabularies designed to represent workflow centric Research Objects. A Research Object is a bundle of semantically described resources created to support a certain publication (e.g., datasets, methods, hypothesis, bibliography used). Workflow centric Research Objects represent the workflows described in a scientific paper, their provenance and their evolution.

Check them out here!

The Workflow Motif Ontology (2012)

Ontology for describing Workflow Motifs. Workflow Motifs outline the kinds of data-intensive activities that are observed in workflows (data-operation motifs) and the different manners in which activities are implemented within workflows (workflow-oriented motifs).

Check it out here!

The Workflow Invocation Ontology (2012)

The Workflow Invocation Ontology (Wf-invoc) is a simple extension of the P-plan ontology to describe how workflow steps are invoked within a workflow execution.

Check it out here!

The P-Plan Ontology (2012)

PROV-O extension for linking Plans and parts of plans to their respective executions (i.e., to their provenance).

Check it out here!

The PROV Ontology (2012)

The PROV Ontology is the RDF serialization of the W3C Provenance Standard Model (PROV), designed to represent and interchange provenance in the Web.

Check it out here!

The Open Provenance Model for Workflows (2011)

The Open Provenance Model for Workflows (OPMW) is an Open Provenance Model Vocabulary (OPMV) profile designed to model the executions and definitions of scientific workflows.

Check it out here!

The AEMET Ontology Network (contributor)(2011)

Ontology network for describing the meteorological information of the Agencia Estatal de Meteorología Española (AEMET). The ontology extends the Sensor Network Ontology (SSN) to represent the Spanish weather stations and their measurements.

Check it out here!

The El Viajero Ontology (2011)

El Viajero is a simple Open Provenance Model (OPM) profile for tracking provenance in a news and blogs scenario. The extension also handles modeling travels, routes and their provenance.

Check it out here!
